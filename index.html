ğŸ“‹ Understanding FERPA

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is the main U.S. federal law that protects the privacy of student education records in any school, college, or training program that receives U.S. Department of Education funds.

ğŸ“š Education Record Definition

"Records, files, documents, and other materials that contain information directly related to a student and are maintained by an educational agency or institution." â€” FERPA statute

ğŸ“Š Examples of Education Records

  • Grades, transcripts, test scores
  • Disability or IEP documents
  • Disciplinary reports
  • Attendance logs tied to a name
  • Student communications and emails
  • Financial aid records

ğŸš« NOT Education Records

  • Personal notes by teachers (not shared)
  • Directory information (if disclosed)
  • Alumni records (after graduation)
  • Employment records for student workers
  • Medical records (HIPAA protected)

âš ï¸ AI and FERPA: The Challenge

When using AI tools with student data, you must ensure FERPA compliance. Most AI platforms store and process data on external servers, which can violate privacy requirements.

ğŸ§¹ Step 1: Strip Names/Direct Identifiers

Remove all personally identifiable information (PII) before uploading to AI systems. This includes names, student IDs, addresses, and other direct identifiers.

ğŸ·ï¸ Step 2: Use Placeholders

Replace identifiers with generic placeholders (Student A, Student B) to preserve context while maintaining anonymity. This allows AI to help while staying FERPA-compliant.

ğŸ’¾ Step 3: Save AI Output Locally First

Stage AI-generated content on a secure device to double-check for hidden PII before sharing or uploading, preventing FERPA violations downstream.
